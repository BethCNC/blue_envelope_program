Building Stronger Communities Through Better Communication

The Blue Envelope Program is a proven, voluntary communication tool that helps law enforcement officers serve our community members with autism more effectively. Developed in partnership with law enforcement agencies, it creates safer, more positive interactions for everyone.

Join 17 states and hundreds of communities who have already implemented this successful program, improving community relations and officer safety.

Learn How It Works See Visual Impact

Purpose

The purpose of the program is to promote inclusivity and enhanced communication and accommodation between law enforcement, emergency services, and community services personnel and community members with a condition or disability where additional accommodations or awareness could be helpful.

Program Benefits

For Community Members

  • Reduces anxiety during traffic stops
  • Provides clear communication guidance
  • Ensures officers understand your needs
  • Completely voluntary participation

For Law Enforcement

  • Immediate recognition of communication needs
  • Clear guidance for appropriate responses
  • Reduces misunderstandings and conflicts
  • Helps comply with ADA requirements

For Communities

  • Promotes inclusivity and accessibility
  • Builds trust between law enforcement and community
  • Reduces potential for negative interactions
  • Creates safer communities for everyone

Visual Impact: Why This Matters

The Blue Envelope

A simple, effective communication tool that helps officers understand and accommodate individuals with autism during traffic stops.

Everyday Reality

Traffic stops are routine for officers but can be overwhelming for autistic individuals due to sensory sensitivities and communication differences.

Legal Framework

The Americans with Disabilities Act requires law enforcement to provide reasonable accommodations and effective communication.

Understanding the Challenge

Traffic stops can be overwhelming for anyone, but especially for individuals with autism who may experience sensory overload from lights, sirens, and rapid communication.

How It Works

1

Obtain Materials

Get a blue envelope and information card from participating law enforcement agencies or community organizations.

2

Prepare Documents

Place copies of your driver's license, vehicle registration, and insurance card inside the envelope.

3

Display When Needed

Present the blue envelope or display the logo during traffic stops to help officers understand your communication needs.
