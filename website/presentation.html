The Gap isVisible.

Union County has the population, the diagnoses, and the policing footprint to need this. It does not have the program.

An estimated nearly six thousand autistic adults drive, walk, and live in Union County. Some of them will be stopped by Union County or Monroe Police this year. None of those officers have been trained on what an autistic meltdown looks like, because no such training exists in this jurisdiction. There is no public policy, no public statement, no public framework. The Blue Envelope program closes that gap with one piece of paper, one 30-minute roll-call briefing, and a one-time material cost of $611.

04 / NC Precedent

Dare CountyDid It First.

North Carolina already has a working, voluntary, county-level Blue Envelope program. It launched in 2024 with no new law and no state funding.

One person. Outer Banks Health Speech Language Pathologist Kristin Kuhar saw the program in another state and brought it home.

Seven agencies. Within months she built a coalition of every police department in Dare County plus the Sheriff's Office.

One year. The program launched in July 2024 with free envelopes at police lobbies, libraries, medical practices, senior centers, and high schools.

No new law required. Voluntary. Self-implemented. Already running. The Dare County model is the template Union County can copy verbatim.

05 / How It Works & What It Costs

Three Steps.Six Hundred Bucks.

The driver does two. The officer does one. The whole program costs less than a good office chair to launch.

01

Pick Up

Driver picks up a free Blue Envelope at any participating Union County PD lobby, library, or DMV pop-up event. No registration, no proof of diagnosis required.

02

Prepare

Driver places copies of license, registration, and insurance inside the envelope, plus optional emergency contacts and personal communication notes.

03

Present

During a stop, the driver hands the envelope to the officer. The officer immediately knows to slow down, reduce sensory input, and use single-step instructions.

06 / Implementation Roadmap

Twelve Weeks FromKickoff to Launch.

Five phases. No legislation required. Every step has a working precedent in Dare County.

  • 01
    Weeks 1–2
    Stakeholder Convening

    Convene the Sheriff's Office, all municipal police chiefs (Monroe, Indian Trail, Waxhaw, Wesley Chapel, Marvin, Weddington, Stallings, Hemby Bridge, Lake Park, Mineral Springs, Wingate, Marshville), and community partners. Adopt a voluntary, no-registry policy framework.

  • 02
    Weeks 3–5
    Materials Production

    Design and print #10 Blue Envelopes with bilingual driver and officer tip sheets. Source through approved county vendor or use 123Print bulk benchmark ($0.35/unit).

  • 03
    Weeks 4–7
    Officer Training Prep

    Develop a 30–45 minute roll-call briefing for every sworn officer. Use Dare County's existing material as the template. Add Union County's own jurisdictional context.

  • 04
    Weeks 8–10
    Distribution & Public Launch

    Stock all PD lobbies, libraries, and DMV events with envelopes. Joint press conference with the Sheriff and at least three municipal chiefs. Press release to local outlets.

  • 05
    Weeks 11–12
    Metrics & Annual Report

    Track distribution counts, training completion rates, and community survey feedback. Publish a one-page annual public report. Schedule next year's review cycle.
