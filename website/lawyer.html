01 / The Stop That Started This

The traffic stop was traumatic.

At the December 29, 2024 stop, I had a documented autistic meltdown on the side of the road — fetal position, rocking, unable to speak, overwhelmed by lights, sirens, and rapid commands. Body cam footage exists. It will show a person in autistic crisis, not a person being uncooperative.

The experience was so disorienting and frightening that I went home and started researching autism and traffic stops, trying to understand what had just happened to me — and how to keep it from happening to other autistic drivers in the same county.

02 / What That Research Became

I built a thing.

My research — and, in fairness, my autistic super-brain refusing to drop the topic for several weeks — led me to the Blue Envelope Program. It is a voluntary tool that helps law enforcement officers communicate effectively with autistic drivers during stops. Six U.S. states have passed laws creating these programs. Three more are pending. Dare County, North Carolina launched its own program in 2024, started by a single Speech Language Pathologist with a coalition of seven local agencies.

Union County does not have anything like this. So I built a complete advocacy proposal for Union County — with sourced data, a real cost model, an officer training module, and an implementation roadmap. It is live at:

blueenvelopeunion.vercel.app

The site has eight sections covering the problem, national adoption, Union County’s autism population, the Dare County NC precedent, the real cost (under $1,000), how the program works, an implementation roadmap, and a separate officer training one-sheet.
