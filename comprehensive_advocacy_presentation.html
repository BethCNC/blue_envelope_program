Blue Envelope Program: Data-Driven Advocacy Presentation

Executive Summary

The Blue Envelope Program is a simple, cost-effective solution to improve police interactions with autistic individuals. This presentation provides data-driven evidence of the program's benefits and implementation feasibility.

20% of autistic adults have police contact by age 21
7x more likely to interact with police than general population

1. The Problem: Scope and Impact

Police Interactions with Autistic Individuals: The Scope of the Problem

Key Statistics:

  • 20% of autistic adults have been questioned by police by age 21
  • 5% of autistic adults have been arrested by age 21
  • 7 times more likely to interact with police than general population
  • 33-50% of use-of-force fatalities involve people with disabilities
  • 40% of prison population has some form of disability (vs. 15% general population)

5. Implementation Examples and Best Practices

Implementation Examples by State

Connecticut Model

First State Implementation (2020)

DMV-driven program that became the national model. Envelopes distributed through DMV locations statewide.

Massachusetts Approach

Legislative Support (2023)

Senate bill S.2558 codifies program. Envelopes available through RMV and police barracks.

Local Program Success

New Jersey Counties

Widespread local adoption starting with Hunterdon County pilot in 2019. Many sheriff offices now distribute envelopes.

7. Ease of Implementation

Low Cost

Minimal financial investment required. Envelopes can be produced locally or through existing partnerships.

Simple Training

Basic officer training on autism awareness and communication strategies. Can be integrated into existing training programs.

Quick Implementation

Program can be launched within weeks of decision. No complex infrastructure or technology required.

Strong Community Support

Autism organizations and families are eager to support implementation. High community buy-in expected.

8. Legal Framework and Compliance

Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Requirements:

  • Law enforcement must provide reasonable accommodations during interactions
  • Effective communication is required for all individuals
  • Procedures must be modified when necessary for safety and accessibility
  • Disability-related behaviors must be recognized and accommodated

How Blue Envelope Program Supports ADA Compliance:

  • Provides advance notice of communication needs
  • Facilitates reasonable modifications to standard procedures
  • Reduces likelihood of misunderstanding disability-related behaviors
  • Supports effective communication requirements
  • Helps prevent discrimination during police interactions
