### 🎯 **Executive Summary**
This document provides comprehensive data-driven evidence supporting the implementation of the Blue Envelope Program in Monroe, North Carolina. The analysis includes national statistics, program effectiveness data, and specific findings about the Monroe Police Department's current awareness level.

## 📊 **National Problem Statistics**

### **Police Interactions with Autistic Individuals**
- **20%** of autistic adults have been questioned by police by age 21
- **5%** of autistic adults have been arrested by age 21
- **7 times more likely** to interact with police than general population
- **33-50%** of use-of-force fatalities involve people with disabilities
- **40%** of prison population has some form of disability (vs. 15% general population)

### **Source Data**
- Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders (2016)
- U.S. Department of Justice statistics
- The Marshall Project analysis
- Prison Policy Initiative data

## 🏛️ **Legal Framework**

### **ADA Requirements:**
The Americans with Disabilities Act requires law enforcement to:
- Provide reasonable accommodations during interactions
- Ensure effective communication with individuals with disabilities
- Modify policies and procedures to avoid discrimination
- Recognize disability-related behaviors and accommodate them

### **How Blue Envelope Program Supports ADA Compliance:**
- Provides advance notice of communication needs
- Facilitates reasonable modifications to standard procedures
- Reduces likelihood of misunderstanding disability-related behaviors
- Supports effective communication requirements
- Helps prevent discrimination during police interactions
